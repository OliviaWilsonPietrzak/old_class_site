Step 1: Setting Up.

I was able to successfully download the QSF file from Dr. Ho’s website, then load the questionnaire into Qualtrics.

Step 2: Analyze the survey.

  1. How is the survey structured?

    The survey begins with a disclosure statement. The survey then proceeds through a variety of questions (matrix, multiple choice, drop down selections), before a conclusion page thanking the participant.

  2. What is the questionnaire composed of?

    The first question is a matrix question with 5 point Likert scale.

    The next few questions are a combination of multiple choice questions (ranging from binary to up to 5 options) and drop down answers.

  3. How are the questions ordered?

    The questions escalate in sensitivity, going from broad questions regarding personal media and purchasing preferences to questions about an individual’s personal identity and socioeconomic status.

  4. Consider importing additional libraries (demographics and zip code).

    I was able to add in the additional libraries. The demographics was interesting in particular – there wasn’t an “all-inclusive” option – an option for Hispanic/Latino was not present in the larger list of racial backgrounds. I ended up adding in a question before the race group selection that allowed an individual to select whether or not they were Hispanic/Latino.

  5. Formatting.

    I was able to change the appearance to the UTD Theme, as well as adding in the Skip Logic to question 4. I tried to “chunk” the questions together to save the participant from having to scroll too much, but while keeping similar questions together. I also formatted the matrix to be mobile-friendly.

    To improve respondents’ experience, I would try to reduce the number of questions – some feel a little redundant.
